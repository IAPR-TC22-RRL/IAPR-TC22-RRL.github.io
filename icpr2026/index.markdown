For the first time in ICPR 2026, the IAPR [Technical Committee 22 (TC-22)](https://iapr-tc22.org/) proposes to acknowledge with a **Reproducible Research Label** badge those papers that have been accepted to the main conference and that, after a specific review of the associated code, are considered eligible to be labeled as [_reproducible_](https://www.acm.org/publications/policies/artifact-review-and-badging-current).

## Overview of the submission and review process

The review process is the following:

1. Each **author**{: .author} of an accepted paper who has checked the "RRPR badge" when submitting the paper to the main conference **may apply**{: .author} for the RRPR badge by **creating a new issue**{: .author} on the [dedicated GitHub repository](https://github.com/IAPR-TC22-RRL/ICPR-2026-Reproducible-Research-Label-submissions-test/). This issue must include some details, in particular:
    - a URL of the **Git repository**{: .author} containing the source code that was used to obtain the results of the paper,
    - an indication of the elements of the paper that the authors consider the reviewers should check.
2. The **ICPR Reproducible Research Chairs**{: .chairs} use the same system as for the papers ([CMT](https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ICPR2026/)) to **assign reviewers to each created issue**{: .chairs}.
3. Each **reviewer**{: .reviewer} attempts to **compile and execute the source code**{: .reviewer} and reproduce the results reported by the authors.
    - As part of the review process, the reviewer **completes a "review form"**{: .reviewer}.
    - At the end, the reviewers **submit the review form**{: .reviewer} on the [CMT](https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ICPR2026/) system.
4. Finally, the ICPR Reproducibility Chairs act as meta-reviewers and **decide on RRPR badge attribution**{: .chairs}.

**The list of labeled papers will appear in a section of the ICPR official proceedings** dedicated to the label, as well as on the websites of ICPR 2026 and TC22.
